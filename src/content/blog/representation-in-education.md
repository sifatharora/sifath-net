Growing up, I rarely saw my culture reflected in the textbooks or classroom discussions at school. That experience is part of why I started writing about education and culture — and why I participated in Sikh Advocacy Day.

## What advocacy day taught me

Speaking with state legislators was intimidating at first, but it showed me that students have a voice in policy discussions. I advocated for inclusive education and Sikh representation in curriculum because I believe every student deserves to see themselves in what they learn.

## Why it matters

When students see their culture, history, and contributions represented in school, it builds confidence and belonging. But inclusive education isn't just for minority students — it helps everyone develop empathy and a more accurate understanding of the world.

This is ongoing work, and I'm committed to being part of it.
